Animals

In this section we talk about animals

Animals are multicellular eukaryotic organisms that form the biological kingdom Animalia. With few exceptions, animals consume organic material, breathe oxygen, are able to move, can reproduce sexually, and grow from a hollow sphere of cells, the blastula, during embryonic development.

Birds

In section2 we talk about birds

Birds are vertebrates (animals with backbones) with wings and feathers. ... But a few bird species do not have strong enough wings to fly, and so these birds are flightless. Birds' bodies are covered with a light, tough layer of feathers and they have very light skeletons.

Humans

In section1 we talk about human

A human is a member of the species Homo sapiens, which means 'wise man' in Latin. Carolus Linnaeus put humans in the mammalian order of primates. Humans are a type of hominid, and chimpanzees, gorillas and orangutans are their closest living relatives. Humans are mammals. They are also social animals.

Space

In section4 we talk about space

Space is a hard vacuum, meaning it is a void containing very little matter. The space between galaxies is not completely empty but has an average of one atom per cubic meter. There are an estimated 100-400 billion stars in our galaxy, the Milky Way.
